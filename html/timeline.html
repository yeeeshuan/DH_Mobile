40s

1941:

Born in a valley near Lake Constance, the Salem Valley, in Germany close to the Swiss border.

50s

1954:

Moved with his family to Lisbon.

1958:

Began a 2 year course at
the Merz academy and
learned about hand setting type and printmaking.

60s

1960:

Began a typesetting apprenticeship at Kuwe printing.

1961:

Began Round Composition Project.

1964:

Enrolled at the Basel School of
Design in Switzerland. Met Armin Hoffman and Emil Ruder.

1968:

Invited by Armin Hoffman
to teach at the Basel School of Design.

70s

1972:

Designs Covers for Typographische Monatsblatter


Writes "How Can One Make Swiss Typography"

1974:

Instructor at the Yale University Summer Graphic Design Program in Brissago

1975:

Became a member of the Alliance Graphique Internationale

80s

1981:

Began experimenting with photomechanical processes

90s

1992:

Legibility studies pushing text's capabilities for readability

2000

2000:

Publishes "My Way
to Typography".

2004:

Retires from teaching.

2010

2013:

Recieved the American Institute of Graphic Arts (AIGA) AIGA Medal.

2020

2021:

Passed away at 80.
